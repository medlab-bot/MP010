A Stroma-Sensing Molecular Platform for Spatial Targeting of TGF-Î² Provokes Durable Tumor Immunity

Abstract 
Accumulation of Transforming growth factor-Î² (TGF-Î²) within the immunosuppressive tumor microenvironment (TME) has been proposed as a major barrier to mounting an effective anti-tumor immunity. However, systemically administered TGF-Î² pathway inhibitors have shown limited clinical benefit in enhancing the response to immune checkpoint inhibitors in solid tumors. To overcome these hurdles, we developed a 'TME-sensing' soluble TGF-Î² type II receptor (TÎ²RII)-Fc fusion protein (FEBP-sTÎ²RII) containing a peptide that binds to the Extra Domain B (EDB) of fibronectin, which is expressed specifically in the extracellular matrix of the TME. FEBP-sTÎ²RII was effectively delivered to the TME in aggressive and immune-resistant pancreatic and triple-negative breast cancer models and stimulated tumor regression by eliminating the stromal barrier and potently improving the immune environment through transcriptional reprogramming of cancer-associated fibroblasts and immune cells. Notably, FEBP-sTÎ²RII induced a robust and sustained tumor-specific immune memory response that even extended to epithelial cancers of distinct histology. Importantly, the remarkable efficacy of FEBP-sTÎ²RII is not associated with systemic immune activation or autoimmunity.
